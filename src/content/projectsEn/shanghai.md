## What it is

An **unofficial** conference recap site that organizes the 30 talks from the 2026 AI Vision Forum (Shanghai) into a knowledge asset you can search, browse, and follow thread by thread:

- **Knowledge starmap**: an interactive knowledge graph of 99 entities (people / organizations / concepts / events) and 288 co-occurrence links, with search, filtering, and drag, where clicking a node takes you straight to the entry.
- **Smart minutes**: each talk includes key quotes, infographics, model explainers, and highlight commentary.
- **Knowledge base**: 100+ entity pages (companies / people / concepts / events), with occurrence counts and two-way interlinks.
- **Resource library**: 131 on-site slides (faces redacted).

Pure static HTML / CSS / JS, with no build dependencies and no third-party libraries, deployed to Cloudflare Pages for automatic release.

## Why build it

A conference floor has an extreme information density, but it's scattered across dozens of talks, hundreds of slides, and in-the-moment memory, and a week later it's basically "heard and forgotten." Structuring the minutes, entities, and links into a searchable, interlinked, zoomable site turns "heard and forgotten" into "come back and look it up anytime, and keep digging along the relationship graph."

The AI content is auto-generated by **Feishu Minutes + a large model**, with a transparent sitewide label of "for study and reference only."

## How you can use it

- Want to quickly revisit a talk → the smart-minutes page for key quotes + highlight commentary.
- Want to dig down along a person / company / concept → the knowledge starmap or a knowledge-base entry, following the two-way links.
- Want the raw material → flip through the on-site slides in the resource library.

Best for: practitioners who didn't attend but want to catch up, researchers doing track mapping, and media wanting to write industry notes.
